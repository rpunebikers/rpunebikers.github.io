About PuneBikers

Our Story

Born on Pune's roads. Raised by adventure. Built by brotherhood.

Mission

Why We Ride

It started with a Reddit post, a 300 km Konkan ride, and a stranger's comment that changed everything. Three people who had never met planned a ride in a comment section, showed up, rode together, and posted photos. That was January 2023 — and the spark that lit r/PuneBikers.

Our mission is to build a community where every rider, regardless of experience, feels welcome, safe, and inspired to explore further than they ever thought possible.

We organise rides, promote responsible motorcycling, mentor new riders, and create memories that last a lifetime.

🛣️

History

The Journey So Far

🔥

Jan 2023 — The Spark

One Post. Three Strangers. One Road.

Someone posted about a 300 km Konkan ride on Reddit. A stranger read it, got inspired, and planned a ride with the original poster right in the comment section. A third rider spotted the thread and asked to join. All three showed up, rode to Dhom Dam, and posted photos from the trip — not knowing they had just started something much bigger.

💬

Early 2023 — Reddit to Telegram

A Community Takes Shape

The ride photos sparked a wave of interest. Riders connected through Reddit chat, then moved to Telegram to keep conversations more organised and members anonymous. The group grew fast — soon crossing 60 members.

📲

Feb 2024 — The WhatsApp Move

314 Riders and Counting

As the community grew, requests for a WhatsApp group started pouring in. The group made the move in February 2024, and it stuck. Today the WhatsApp group has 314 active members coordinating rides, sharing routes, and keeping each other safe.

📈

2023–2024 — Reddit Explodes

10,000+ Members on r/PuneBikers

Word spread beyond Pune. Riders across Maharashtra and beyond found the subreddit, shared their own rides, and the community crossed the 10K member milestone — all organic, all grassroots.

⭐

Today

Still Growing. Still Riding.

What began as a comment between strangers is now a 10K+ Reddit community and 314-member WhatsApp group — with regular rides, no hierarchy, and the same spirit as that first trip to Dhom Dam.

Philosophy

How We Ride

🛡️

Safety Is Not Optional

Full gear is mandatory on all group rides. No exceptions. We believe arriving is more important than speed.

🤝

No Rider Left Behind

We ride as a unit. Pace is set by the group, not the fastest rider. Every pit stop waits for the last bike.

🌿

Respect the Road

Leave every place better than you found it. No littering. No reckless riding through villages or wildlife zones.

📖

Always Learning

We run regular riding workshops, first-aid training, and mechanical basics. A better rider is a safer rider.

Safety Culture

Gear Up. Every Time.

Our safety checklist isn't a formality — it's how we make sure everyone comes home.

⛑️

Full-Face Helmet

ISI/DOT certified required

🧥

Riding Jacket

Armoured or padded

🥊

Gloves

Riding-grade, knuckle protection

👢

Riding Boots

Ankle protection minimum
